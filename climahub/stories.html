"We Walked for Water, Now I Speak for My Village"

Fatuma, 37, Turkana

"In 2022, the drought dried our last borehole. My husband died walking for water, and I became a widow with five children..."

"In 2022, the drought dried our last borehole. My husband died walking for water, and I became a widow with five children. I didn't know grief could feel like fire in the chest. For months, I moved through days like a shadow, tending to my children but feeling nothing.

Then a community health worker told me about ClimaHub. At first, I didn't see the point. What would writing change? But one night, when the children were asleep, I began to type on the borrowed tablet. The words poured out - about the dust that filled our lungs, about watching my strong husband grow weak, about the shame of begging for water.

When I shared my story on ClimaHub, something unexpected happened. Women from across Kenya responded. Some shared similar losses. Others offered practical help. A group in Nairobi sent solar-powered water purifiers. But most importantly, I wasn't a victim anymore â€” I was a witness, a warrior, a woman whose voice matters.

Now I organize water truck deliveries to our village and speak at county meetings. The drought took much, but it gave me this: the certainty that my voice can make change."

"Floods Took My Schoolbag, But Not My Future"

"My books floated away when the river broke its banks. The water came so fast we barely had time to grab our baby brother before scrambling onto the roof. For three days we waited there, watching our home fill with brown water.

When we finally got to dry land, everything was gone - my uniform, my exercise books, the English dictionary I'd saved for months to buy. I cried for those books more than our ruined furniture. School was my way out, my chance to become a doctor. Without materials, how would I study?

But I remembered what my mother said â€” 'You carry your future inside you.' ClimaHub gave me a space to write, and strangers from across Kenya read my story. One teacher sent me used books. Another woman paid for my school fees for a term. I'm now writing poems to heal other girls like me.

The floods come more often now, but so does help. Through ClimaHub, I've learned that disaster doesn't have to mean the end of dreams."

"Cyclone Ruth Left Me Homeless â€” ClimaHub Helped Me Find Community"

Mirembe, 29, Busia

"I used to think therapy was for rich people. But after the cyclone, I couldn't sleep or eat. Through ClimaHub's AI chatbot and therapist referrals..."

"I used to think therapy was for rich people. But after the cyclone, I couldn't sleep or eat. For weeks after Cyclone Ruth destroyed our home, I jumped at every sound of wind. My hands shook so badly I couldn't even hold my baby properly.

At the displacement camp, a volunteer told me about ClimaHub's mental health resources. I was skeptical, but desperate. The AI chatbot, Nuru, became my first lifeline. It didn't judge my tears or tell me to 'be strong.' It just listened and taught me simple breathing exercises.

Through ClimaHub, I got connected to a counselor who understood climate trauma. And I met other women online who knew my pain. We formed a support group that still meets weekly, even though we're now rebuilding our homes.

The cyclone took our roof, but it also broke down walls I didn't know I'd built around my heart. Now I help other women access the mental health support I almost didn't seek."

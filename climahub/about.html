About ClimaHub

Humanizing climate data through the lived experiences of women and girls

Our Mission

ClimaHub humanizes climate data through the lived experiences of women and girls in climate-vulnerable communities. We transform pain into power, silence into storytelling, and trauma into collective action.

Founded in 2020, ClimaHub began as a small storytelling project and has grown into a global platform connecting thousands of women affected by climate change. Our work bridges the gap between personal experience and policy change, ensuring that the voices of those most impacted are heard in the halls of power.

Our Core Values

Compassion

We approach every story with empathy and respect, creating a safe space for healing and connection.

Advocacy

We amplify voices that are often silenced, turning personal experiences into powerful calls for change.

Community

We believe in the transformative power of collective healing and action.

Resilience

We honor the strength of survivors and support their journey from victim to leader.

Meet Our Team

Bahati Kahindi

Founder & Executive Director

A climate activist born and raised in Malindi, Bahati watched coastal floods displace hundreds in her community, including her own cousins. She founded ClimaHub to ensure women like them are not erased. With a background in sustainable fashion, gender advocacy, and community mental health, she now leads ClimaHub with compassion and conviction.

Bahati's work has been recognized by the UN Women and she was named one of Africa's 100 Most Influential Women in Climate Action by Forbes Africa.

Priscilla Jonathan

Communications Lead

A former radio journalist turned storyteller, Priscilla believes in the radical power of women's voices. After covering a drought survival camp in Turkana, she joined ClimaHub to ensure these stories don't vanish into silence. She crafts campaigns that echo in parliament halls and healing circles alike.

Priscilla holds a Master's in Media for Social Change and has trained over 500 women in digital storytelling techniques across East Africa.
